The Hidden Problem Beneath Every Mine

Mining environments are exposed to continuous vibration from drilling, heavy machinery, blasting, and geological shifts. These micro-vibrations often serve as pre-failure indicators for collapses, slope instability, equipment failure and other catastrophic events.

Key problems

  • Insufficient continuous monitoring — many mines rely on periodic human inspections.
  • Micro-vibrations are invisible to traditional systems yet signal early failure.
  • Tailings and structural failures cause catastrophic environmental and human costs.
  • Scaling operations increases risk when monitoring does not scale with it.

Real Risk

Micro-vibration patterns often precede structural failures by hours to days.

Low Visibility

Traditional inspection intervals miss crucial pre-failure signals.

High Cost

Unplanned downtime & failures cause large economic and social losses.
